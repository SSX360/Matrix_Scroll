Why not Sigstore or artifact attestations?

Those tools attest build outputs after CI. Matrix Scroll records metadata and signatures before merge, keeping that attestation verifiable offline. See the comparison.

    Why the Hosted Identity Authority? ("You can't fork trust")
    
              
    
                Anyone can copy the open-source SDK or fork the protocol. However, you cannot fork the hosted issuer registry others rely on. The Hosted Identity Authority cryptographically vouches for your key's binding (linking public keys to verified email domains or GitHub profiles) and handles revocation checks in real-time. Without a trusted authority, signature keys remain advisory and are vulnerable to machine-level spoofing.
